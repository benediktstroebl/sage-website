Science of Agent Evaluation

Advancing the systematic study and evaluation of AI agents through rigorous methodologies and open benchmarks.

About SAgE

The Science of Agent Evaluation (SAgE) research group focuses on developing rigorous methodologies for evaluating AI agents. Our work spans benchmark development, evaluation frameworks, and systematic studies of agent capabilities.

Current research directions include cost-controlled evaluations, reproducibility in agent research, and standardized benchmarking frameworks.

Key Projects

HAL (Holistic Agent Leaderboard)

A standardized, third-party leaderboard system for evaluating AI agents across multiple dimensions including accuracy, cost, and reliability. HAL addresses the widespread limitations of current agent evaluation by providing:

  • Standardized evaluation metrics across benchmarks
  • Cost-controlled performance comparisons
  • Verified results with periodic re-running
  • Detailed error analysis and visualization tools
View Project Read Paper →

CORE-bench

A comprehensive benchmark designed to evaluate the reasoning and problem-solving capabilities of AI agents. CORE-bench focuses on:

  • Multi-step reasoning evaluation
  • Real-world problem-solving scenarios
  • Standardized metrics for comparison
  • Extensive test cases and validation

AI Agents That Matter

A comprehensive study analyzing current agent benchmarks and evaluation practices, identifying key shortcomings and proposing solutions for:

  • Cost-controlled evaluation methodologies
  • Benchmark design principles
  • Reproducibility standards
  • Real-world applicability metrics
